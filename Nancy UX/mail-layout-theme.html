Mail Layout and Themes


Yahoo Mail layout was one of the most important aspects of the Mail app, and that is because we have the list of mails along with the working area, in order to compose the messages.

Themes are so critical in Yahoo Mail app, where we give the user the choice to choose the favorite UI appearance.

My contribution includes:
  • Worked on the sturcture of the New Mail app, with different screen sizes and scenarios
  • Collaborated with the other team members regarding competitive analysis among different apps
  • Verified and tested the layout in different screen sizes and devices
  • Worked on different themes variations, from light to dark versions
Layout and Structure:
Grid System:

The grid system in Yahoo Mail is essential as it helps align page elements based on sequential columns and rows. Once we have this structured framework in place and arrange and present content such as text, imagery and graphics in a structured and well-balanced way
Defining your base unit will allow you to create the scale of supported sizes in your spatial system. Looking at different Material Design around the web we noticed different increment systems, like 4px/pt, 8px/pt, etc.
We preferred method of 4px linear scale for elements for spacing icons or small text blocks.This system is meant to reduce confusion but also be easy to implement.





Responsive Layout:

Yahoo Mail responsive design makes the Mail app faster, more accessible, and easier to navigate. It makes it easier for users to then find the emails, and actions they are looking for and typically encourages them to stay active on the Mail app. Essentially, Yahoo Mail responsive design allows the app to automatically scales its content and elements to match the screen size on which it is viewed.


Breakpoints are quite useful for creating a responsive web design, as it offers an awesome user experience on different screen sizes. This is realized by splitting the design into a smaller version of the Yahoo Mail app based on the device’s screen size.

Looking at other apps helped a lot, in order to decide the best way to structure our Yahoo Mail app, it was useful, yet we focused on our own users needs at the first place.

Using different internal tools, we could determine the percentage of users, and the corresponding screen sizes, which was huge, as we could focus on the majority of our users, with still taking into considerations the other screen sizes as well but with less attention.

While working on the layout of Yahoo Mail, we had some fixed widths, and some flexible columns, and that depends on the content, and the requirements of that content. Also we had different layouts for Yahoo Mail, so we needed to make sure we cover all those cases, while making sure the app is responsive.
